My CMU Coursework

I'll be adding to my list of courses and sharing my thoughts on them throughout college. The courses below are ones I've taken/are taking, and my technical classes are bolded. Classes I found particularly well-taught have a star (*) next to them, and classes I found transformative have two stars (**).

Fall 2023 (Freshman)

  • 15-151: Mathematical Foundations of Computer Science **
  • 15-122: Principles of Imperative Programming
  • 21-241: Matrices and Linear Transformations *
  • 84-680: US Grand Strategy


15-151 was my first class on mathematical proofs. It was very challenging but interesting, and I enjoyed the new ways of thinking I was introduced to. Prof. John Mackey is iconic for good reason, and I hope to take another class with him at CMU.

I thought 21-241 was very well organized. Prof. David Offner lectures well, and his class notes were clear and useful. I would definitely recommend taking his class.

I quite enjoyed US Grand Strategy, although I wasn't able to put as much effort in the class as I would've liked because I was constantly busy with other work. 15-122 was a good and thorough course on C programming, but I didn't enjoy it as much as the others.

Spring 2024 (Freshman)

  • 15-251: Great Theoretical Ideas in Computer Science **
  • 15-150: Principles of Functional Programming
  • 21-266: Vector Calculus for Computer Scientists *
  • 76-106: Writing About Literature, Art, and Culture
  • 76-107: Writing about Data


15-251 was the most challenging and fascinating class this semester by far. Both Prof. Ada and Prof. Saad are great, the class is very well structured, and there are lots of resources to help you. This class will require a good deal of outside study time â€” I feel like my understanding of topics came mostly from poring over the class's textbook for hours, and lectures were simply the cherry on top.

21-266 gets a special mention because I took it with Prof. Clive Newstead, who (in my opinion) has got to be one of the best professors at CMU. Similar to my comments about 22-241 (Matrices), the class is extremely organized and also challenging. Clive's homeworks and tests require a deep and thorough understanding of the concepts and are always interesting.

15-150, though it doesn't get any stars, was probably the class whose assignments I actually enjoyed the most. Functional programming was novel and elegant to me, so the homeworks were like fun puzzles. I even applied to TA the class, but was waitlisted.
